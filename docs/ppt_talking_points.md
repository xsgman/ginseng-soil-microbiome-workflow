# PPT Talking Points

## Soil Heatmap

This slide compares KXH and RHB with the matched CK baseline across multiple
soil indicators. To compare indicators with different units, each treatment-CK
difference was divided by the pooled standard deviation of that metric. Orange
means higher than CK and blue means lower than CK. The key point is that KXH
mainly responds in carbon and nutrient indicators, while RHB shows stronger
responses in acidity-related indicators.

## Soil PCA

The previous slide decomposed each soil indicator separately. This PCA slide
combines all indicators to ask whether the integrated soil environmental state
separates among CK, KXH, and RHB. PC1 and PC2 are weighted combinations of all
standardized indicators. Separation of points indicates that treatment effects
are not only single-index changes but an integrated soil-state shift.

## Ginseng Phenotype

This slide shows phenotype and disease responses after direction harmonization.
Positive values mean improvement relative to CK. Most points are on the right of
zero, indicating favorable trends. Biomass-related traits show larger effects,
whereas disease index changes are smaller and should be described as a trend
unless supported by p-values.

## Metagenomics

This slide moves from soil and phenotype to the root-zone microbiome. The PCoA
panel shows community-structure shifts relative to CK. The functional heatmap
summarizes functional potentials related to carbon use, transport,
colonization, motility, biofilm, and stress adaptation. These are functional
potentials and require targeted validation.

## Bacillus Rationale

Bacillus shows a positive response, but the response can be limited under field
conditions. This suggests that beneficial bacteria still face survival and
colonization pressure in replanted soil. Therefore, carrier protection and
controlled release are needed to improve persistence and functional stability.
